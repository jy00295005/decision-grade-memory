# Style Guide

## Target tone

Academic, precise, restrained, and non-promotional.

## Paragraph style

Use compact paragraphs that make one clear point at a time. Prefer clarity over rhetorical flourish.

## Section-writing style

Open each section by stating its purpose, then develop only evidence-backed or clearly marked placeholder content.

## Citation discipline

Only cite real sources. If a citation is missing, add `TODO:` rather than a placeholder reference.

## Wording to avoid

Avoid hype, certainty without support, vague novelty claims, and marketing language such as "revolutionary," "game-changing," or "best-in-class."

## How to mark uncertainty

Use phrases such as "working placeholder," "provisional framing," or `TODO:` when the concept is not yet settled.

## How to mark missing evidence

Use `TODO: add source`, `TODO: verify claim`, or `TODO: support with empirical evidence`.
