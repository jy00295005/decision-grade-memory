# Figure 3 Prompt

Use this prompt to generate the semantic structure figure for the decision episode.

```text
Create a publication-quality academic figure titled:

"Structure of a Decision Episode"

Goal:
Design a clear, information-dense conceptual figure for a research paper on scenario memory for SME decision support.

Style:
- clean academic infographic
- white background
- soft muted colors
- publication-ready
- polished and readable
- not cartoonish
- not corporate marketing style
- balanced visual hierarchy
- minimal but semantically rich

Layout:
Use a structured semantic diagram, not a radial “all nodes point to center” layout.

Center:
- Decision Episode
- subtitle inside the box:
  "A bounded unit for decision-grade context construction"

Top row (context and intent):
- Actor / Business Context
- Goal
- Constraint
- Trigger / Challenge

Bottom row (response and consequence):
- Action / Decision
- Outcome
- Evidence Bundle
- Temporal Context

Use arrows with semantic labels:
- Actor / Business Context -> Decision Episode
  label: "defines operating context"
- Goal -> Decision Episode
  label: "defines objective"
- Trigger / Challenge -> Goal
  label: "threatens"
- Constraint -> Action / Decision
  label: "shapes"
- Trigger / Challenge -> Decision Episode
  label: "initiates"
- Decision Episode -> Action / Decision
  label: "organizes response"
- Action / Decision -> Outcome
  label: "leads to"
- Evidence Bundle -> Outcome
  label: "supports interpretation"
- Temporal Context -> Decision Episode
  label: "situates in time"

Design requirements:
- avoid duplicate nodes
- keep spacing even and uncluttered
- labels must be legible at paper size
- make the semantic flow obvious
- emphasize that the episode is more than a collection of fragments
- show that outcome and evidence are linked, not isolated
- ensure the figure looks suitable for an AI / knowledge graph / decision support paper

Footer sentence at the bottom of the figure:
"Scenario memory encodes why a decision was taken, under which constraints, and with what evidence-backed outcome."

Important:
Do not make it look like a poster or sketch note.
Make it look like a serious journal/conference paper figure.
```
