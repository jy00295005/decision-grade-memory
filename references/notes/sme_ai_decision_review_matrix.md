# SME AI Decision Review Matrix

| Paper / source | Domain | Problem addressed | Type of AI support | Memory / context mechanism | Limitation | Relevance to scenario-memory hypothesis |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Oldemeyer, Jede, and Teuteberg (2025), `Investigation of artificial intelligence in SMEs` | Industrial SMEs | AI adoption and implementation barriers | Broad AI adoption across business functions | Not a memory paper; focuses on organizational and technical preconditions | Strong on barriers, weak on decision-support architecture | Indirectly relevant: suggests SMEs face knowledge, data, ROI, and maturity constraints before richer decision support is possible |
| Schwaeke et al. (2025), `The new normal: The status quo of AI adoption in SMEs` | SMEs across functions | State of AI adoption in SMEs | Broad AI adoption | Context framed via TOE-style clusters, not memory design | Focuses on readiness and adoption conditions, not decision-grade context reconstruction | Indirectly relevant: helps separate adoption barriers from architectural gaps |
| Hansen and Bøgh (2021), `AI and IoT in SMEs: A survey` | Manufacturing SMEs | Predictive analytics and Industry 4.0 uptake | AI plus IoT in SME operations | Data collected from machines and operations; no explicit episodic or scenario memory | Emphasizes novelty, knowledge gaps, and limited exploitation | Indirectly relevant: shows data capture exists, but decision context remains underdeveloped |
| Adomako (2022), `Uncertainty and decision making in small firms` | Small firms | Decision-making under uncertainty | Not an AI-support paper | No explicit memory mechanism | Does not specify AI architecture; broader entrepreneurship framing | Relevant for problem framing: SME decisions are uncertainty-heavy and likely sensitive to incomplete context |
| Brereton et al. (2023), `AI model documentation in translational science` | Clinical decision support | Translation barriers for AI-enabled support tools | AI/ML decision support with documentation requirements | Documentation and traceability rather than memory | Domain is healthcare, not SMEs; still shows deployment barriers from poor transparency and traceability | Relevant analogue: decision support may fail without transparent context and rationale records |
| Joren et al. (2025), `Sufficient Context` | RAG systems | Whether retrieved context is enough for reliable answering | Retrieval-augmented generation | Retrieved context evaluated for sufficiency | Shows that models often answer incorrectly when context is insufficient | Strongly relevant: fragment retrieval alone does not guarantee decision-grade support |
| Wang et al. (2024), `Agent Workflow Memory` | LLM agents | Long-horizon tasks with complex action trajectories | Agent support using reusable workflows | Workflow memory learned from prior trajectories | Benchmarks are web tasks, not business decisions | Strongly relevant: suggests reusable procedural memory can outperform plain retrieval for multi-step tasks |
| Fountas et al. (2025), `Human-inspired Episodic Memory for Infinite Context LLMs` | LLM memory | Long-context reasoning and retrieval | Event-based episodic memory for LLMs | Episodic events plus temporally contiguous retrieval | General long-context benchmark paper, not enterprise decision support | Strongly relevant conceptually: supports event- and episode-based memory as an alternative to fragment retrieval |
| Wu et al. (2025), `Retrieval Augmented Decision-Making` | Structured decision support | Multi-criteria decisions from fragmented documents | RAG plus multi-criteria decision framework | Structured weighting and traceable reasoning chains | Early arXiv paper; not SME-specific | Very relevant: explicitly states ordinary RAG lacks weighting and traceable reasoning for decision support |
| Park et al. (2022), `Explainable Predictive Decision Mining for Operational Support` | Business processes / BPM | Predicting and explaining decisions in process flows | Predictive operational support | Event-log and decision-trace context | BPM framing, not LLM or SME-specific | Relevant adjacent concept: process-aware context and explanation may be part of scenario reconstruction |
| Bouslama et al. (2015), `Traceability of the reasoning` | Teleexpertise | Ensuring traceable collaborative decision-making | Semantic decision framework | Explicit reasoning traceability | Older and cross-domain | Relevant analogue: traceability may be a missing component alongside memory |
| Hansen, Meservy, and Zwick (1995), `Case-Based Reasoning: Application Techniques for Decision Support` | Decision support systems | Reusing prior decisions to support current decisions | Case-based reasoning | Case memory of prior situations and solutions | Older pre-LLM literature; not SME-specific | Relevant foundational concept: scenario memory may overlap with modernized case memory |
